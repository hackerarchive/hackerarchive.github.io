This is a private, independent archive. It is not affiliated with Anonymous or any political organization. The purpose is documentation and preservation of digital culture, mods, incidents and underground history for research and archival reference.

Why Digital Archives Matter

Digital history disappears faster than physical records. Forums shut down, repositories vanish, links rot and accounts are deleted. Without archives, context is lost. This project exists to preserve what would otherwise be erased.

Underground ≠ Criminal

Underground communities include modders, researchers, archivists and hobbyists. Not all underground activity is malicious—much of it is experimental, educational and cultural.

On Mods, Ownership and Preservation

Mods extend the lifespan of games and software. Preservation requires careful handling, clear attribution and respect for creators while documenting historical context.

Linux Hardening Basics

• Disable root SSH login
• Use key-based authentication
• Enable firewall (ufw/nftables)
• Audit logs with auditd
• Keep attack surface minimal

Basic OPSEC for Online Privacy

• Separate identities and roles
• Avoid account reuse
• Use password managers
• Understand metadata leakage
• Layer defenses; no single solution

Networking Fundamentals

Understand TCP/IP, DNS, routing, ports and NAT. Most incidents originate from misconfiguration rather than advanced exploitation.

Secure Backups & Archiving

Maintain offline backups, verify integrity with hashes, keep multiple mirrors and document provenance to ensure long-term access.
